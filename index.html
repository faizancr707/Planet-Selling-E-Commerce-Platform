Mercury

Mercury, the smallest planet in our solar system, holds a profound narrative of endurance and extremes. Situated closest to the Sun, it endures relentless bombardment by solar radiation, with temperatures soaring to nearly 430°C (800°F) during the day, only to plunge to a frigid -180°C (-290°F) at night.
Mercury’s surface tells a tale of ancient violence and cosmic artistry, scarred by craters and vast basins like the Caloris Basin, a relic of an asteroid impact so powerful it created seismic waves that reverberated through the planet, shaping its far side. Its cracked plains and cliffs, known as lobate scarps, hint at a shrinking planet, contracting as its iron-rich core cools and solidifies—a testament to its dynamic interior.
